💀 1. Severely Underweight (BMI < 16)

    🔴 What it means:

  • You are way below your ideal body mass.
  • Serious risk of organ weakness,
    immune deficiency, bone damage, and even heart issues.

    💪 What to do:

  • Eat calorie-dense, healthy food:
  • Nut butters, nuts, paneer, ghee,
    banana shake, sweet potatoes, oats, whole milk
    (if you are not lactose-intolerant).
  • Eat every 3-5 hours.
  • Strength training to build mass, not just cardio.
  • Track your weight weekly, not daily.

🟠 2. Moderately Underweight (BMI 16.0 – 16.9)

    ⚠️ What it means:

  • Your body still lacks enough fat/muscle to support you properly.
  • Energy drops, fatigue, brittle nails, skin issues, etc.

    💪 What to do:

  • Same strategy as above, but increase meal frequency and protein intake
    (e.g., eggs, lentils, tofu, chicken).
  • Add sattu, peanut butter toast, boiled eggs, oats+milk combo.
  • Workout 3x/week, focus on hypertrophy.

🟡 3. Mildly Underweight (BMI 17 – 18.4)

    🌤️ What it means:

  • You are close to normal but still low.
    May have fatigue, immunity issues.

    💪 What to do:

  • Eat in slight surplus (300–500 extra calories / day).

    • • Include:

  • Rice + dal + ghee
  • Bananas + nuts
  • Protein shakes post-workout ( if you do workout then )
  • Do light strength training and slowly gain muscle.

✅ 4. Normal / Healthy (BMI 18.5 – 24.9)

    🟢 What it means:

    • You’re at a healthy weight. Best for:

  • Physical performance
  • Disease prevention
  • Energy, mood, sleep

    ✅ What to do:

  • Maintain balance: 80% clean food, 20% flexible.
  • Stay active: walk, stretch, train 3–5 time / week.
  • Sleep 7–8 hrs / day
  • Protein intake: 1.2–2g / kg body weight to maintain muscle

🟠 5. Overweight (BMI 25 – 29.9)

    ⚠️ What it means:

    • Not critical, but risk of:

  • Heart disease
  • Blood pressure
  • Belly fat-related inflammation

    💪 What to do:

  • Caloric deficit: Eat ~300–500 kcal less than maintenance
  • High protein diet to prevent muscle loss
  • Cut sugar, fried stuff, and late-night snacks
  • Add walking (10k steps/day) + basic strength workouts

🔴 6. Obese Class I (BMI 30 – 34.9)

    🚨 What it means:

    • You are officially obese. Fat around organs increases risk of:

  • Type 2 diabetes
  • Joint pain
  • Sleep apnea
  • Low testosterone (for men)

    💪 What to do:

  • Strict calorie tracking
  • Eat clean meals: roti, dal, veggies, egg whites, oats, chana, sattu
  • Workout every day, even just walking counts
  • Drink 3–4L water/day, avoid soft drinks completely

🔥 7. Obese Class II (BMI 35 – 39.9)

    🛑 What it means:

  • Higher risk of organ failure, hormonal imbalance, insulin resistance

    💪 What to do:

  • High protein, low sugar, zero processed food
  • Break weight loss into phases: aim for 5kg loss at a time
  • Walk, stair climb, shadow box — build cardio endurance
  • Avoid extreme diets — focus on sustainability

☠️ 8. Obese Class III (BMI 40+) – EXTREME

    🚨 CRITICAL:

  • High risk of stroke, cardiac events, cancer, even early death.
  • But you can turn it around with discipline —
    people have done it and lived to inspire the world.

    💪 Battle Plan:

    • Doctor check-up ( Optional but ideal )

    • Start small: and increase with time ( GRADUALY )

  • Walk 15–20 mins/day
  • Drink more water
  • Eat homemade meals
  • Avoid junk, sweets, bread, pasta
  • Keep workouts low impact first (walking, bodyweight)
  • Then slowly add strength training, boxing, resistance bands
